Convex Optimization Based Algorithms for Clustering Large Graphs

  • October 2012 - ongoing
  • With: Prof. Babak Hassibi, Samet Oymak
  • Casts clustering in unweighted and weighted graphs in large social networks as convex optimization problem to provide provable guarantees, efficient algorithms, and support for missing data.

Citation Centrality and Academia Salaries

  • March 2012 - May 2012
  • With: Prof. Adam Wiermann, Kijun Seo, Michael Hirshleifer
  • We analyzed 18 years of citation data in Theoretical High Energy Physics and salary data of professors.
  • We investigated whether measures of productivity based on citation centrality and seniority could explain academic salaries.
  • We discussed our observations and offered reasons why sophisticated measures (like h- and g- indices) do no predict salary.

Some models in population dynamics: Stability, limit cycles, chaos and implications on control

  • Jan 2011 - April 2011
  • With: Prof. Gaurav Raina, Arun Chaganty
  • We analyzed how two simple population models, operating across two time scales, behave.
  • We showed that they capture the range of phenomenon from fixed points, to limit cycles, to chaos, and analytically characterise the same using Hopf bifurcation theory.
  • We commented on the qualitative differences between the two models, and the resulting implications to control theory.

A study of Analytical Approximations for the Performance Analysis of IEEE 802.11 Wireless Local Area Networks

  • Summer Research Project: May - July 2010
  • With: Prof. Anurag Kumar
  • Application of Mean Field Limit Based Approximations to wireless networks
    • Analysed the behavior of Scaled Node Attempt Model of a wireless network.
    • Simulated the system and compared the results with the approximate ODE solutions.
    • Analysed the local stability of the equilibrium point and proved the existence of a zero eigen value for a general scaled node attempt model.
  • Analysis of IEEE 802.11n Infrastructure Wireless Local Area Netowrs with Channel Errors
    • Analysed the 802.11 WLAN with channel errors and showed that the sufficient condition for the existence of unique fixed point for an error-less system holds good for the system with channel errors also.
    • Modeled a Single Cell IEEE 802.11n Infrastructure WLAN with two class of nodes, one with good channel conditions and the other with channel errors.

Applications of Generalized Discrete Fourier Transforms in OFDM
